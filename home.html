The Author

Rick Riordan, the #1 New York Times bestselling author, has written over twenty novels for young readers, including the widely acclaimed Percy Jackson series.

Before becoming a full-time writer,... Riordan taught English and history at middle schools in the San Francisco Bay Area and Texas for 15 years. During his time teaching in San Antonio, he received the Master Teacher Award from Saint Mary's Hall.

Riordan initially wrote mystery novels for adults while working as a teacher. His Tres Navarre series earned prestigious honors, including the Edgar, Anthony, and Shamus awards. His journey into children's literature began when he created The Lightning Thief as a bedtime story for his eldest son.

Today, Riordan's books have sold over 190 million copies worldwide and have been translated into more than 37 languages. Along with his wife Becky, he serves as an executive producer for TV and film adaptations of his works, such as Percy Jackson, The Kane Chronicles, and Daughter of the Deep.

Now writing full-time, Riordan resides in Boston with his wife and their two sons.
